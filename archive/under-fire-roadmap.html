Data Sources

ACLED
Primary — Armed Conflict Location & Event Data
Free API. Covers rocket/missile attacks with date, location, event type, and actor coding. Global coverage, rigorous methodology. Best source for granular conflict events. Requires free account registration.
Quality
GDELT Project
Supplementary — Global Event Language
Massive global news event database. Use for geopolitical context: finding which political events preceded attack spikes. Enables the "escalation trigger" analysis. BigQuery accessible, free tier sufficient.
Quality
Global Terrorism DB
Supplementary — GTD / START Maryland
Strong historical coverage through 2020. Good for validation and back-filling ACLED gaps in earlier years. Downloadable CSV. Different coding methodology — needs reconciliation with ACLED.
Quality
IDF Public Reports
Validation — Official Statistics
Periodic aggregate counts (e.g. "X rockets fired in Y operation"). Not granular enough for time-series analysis but essential for validating totals. Scraped from IDF Spokesperson and official press releases.
Quality
Wikipedia Timelines
Labels — Conflict Episode Metadata
Well-sourced conflict episode timelines. Use for labelling the timeline (Operation Cast Lead, Pillar of Defence, Guardian of the Walls, Oct 7 war). Provides episode start/end dates and key event anchors.
Quality
UNOCHA
Supplementary — Humanitarian Reports
UN humanitarian situation reports. Contain attack frequency data for specific conflict periods. Good for cross-referencing casualty and displacement data to contextualise attack waves.
Quality

Analytical Questions & Patterns to Find

⬤ Temporal

Time Signatures

Do attacks have a preferred time of day, day of week, or season?

  • Is there a circadian rhythm to launches?
  • Are there day-of-week biases?
  • Do Ramadan periods alter attack frequency?
  • Do attacks cluster in escalation waves with predictable gaps?
  • How long does each "flare-up" cycle last on average?

⬤ Spatial

Geographic Drift

Has rocket reach into Israel expanded over time? Are target patterns stable?

  • How far north/inland have rockets reached each year?
  • Has the "range envelope" expanded since 2008?
  • Are specific cities targeted more than others?
  • Do northern (Hezbollah) and southern (Gaza) fronts differ spatially?
  • Where do rockets land vs. where are they intercepted?

⬤ Actor

Signature Differences

Do Hamas, Hezbollah, PIJ, and Iran direct each have recognisable patterns?

  • Do different actors prefer different times of day?
  • Do attack volumes per episode differ by actor?
  • Has any actor's operational tempo changed over time?
  • How do direct Iranian ballistic strikes differ from proxy rocket attacks?
  • Is there evidence of coordination between simultaneous fronts?

⬤ Correlation

Escalation Triggers

Can spikes be reliably predicted by geopolitical events?

  • Do attacks spike after targeted assassinations?
  • Is there a ceasefire "last gasp" effect before agreements?
  • Do diplomatic breakdowns reliably precede attack waves?
  • How quickly do attacks resume after ceasefires?
  • Is there a "retaliation lag" — time between IDF action and response?

⬤ Capability

Qualitative Leaps

When did rocket capability step-change? Can we see it in the data?

  • When did rockets first reach Tel Aviv? Jerusalem?
  • Is the first use of ballistic missiles (Iran 2024) visible as a discontinuity?
  • Has Iron Dome interception rate changed as volume scales?
  • Do saturation attacks (many simultaneous) appear in data?

⬤ Reporting

Data Bias Checks

What does the data NOT capture that must be disclosed?

  • Are small-scale attacks systematically under-reported?
  • Does media access affect event counts during escalations?
  • Is ACLED coverage consistent across all years?
  • How should we handle "unknown" actor attribution?

Visualisation Components

Conflict Timeline Strip
Zoomable dot-plot. Every event is a circle scaled by intensity. Click any cluster to zoom into that episode.
Attack Clock (Polar)
24-hour radial chart. Attack density by hour of day. The key "signature" viz — instantly reveals time-of-day preferences.
Animated Attack Map
Dots appear at impact sites as time progresses. Animated arcs where origin data exists. Filterable by actor / front.
Escalation Detector
Smoothed rolling-average attack frequency. Auto-highlights escalation periods. Annotated with operation starts and ceasefires.
Actor Comparison Panel
Side-by-side breakdown by actor. Volume, timing, and geographic signature per group. Filterable and sortable.
Range Envelope Chart
How far north and inland did rockets reach each year? Shows the "capability step-change" story — a key qualitative narrative.

Guiding Principles

◎

Data First

Let findings drive the narrative. Don't build a story then find data to fit it. If a pattern isn't in the data, it doesn't go in the site.

◎

Transparent Limits

Every claim needs a source. Every gap needs to be named. A rigorous methodology note is not a weakness — it's credibility.

◎

Show, Don't List

Every insight should be expressed visually before it's expressed in words. If you can't draw it, you don't understand it yet.

◎

Human Scale

Numbers are abstractions. Wherever possible, connect the data to real places and real episodes that readers might remember.
